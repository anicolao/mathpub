# Self-assessment standard

Write directly to the student in brief, natural language.

## After a section

Include:

- a three-choice confidence rating;
- “Which question best shows what you understand? Explain why.”
- a prompt tied to the section’s most important misconception;
- “Which question will you correct, and what will you change?”
- one method-explanation prompt; and
- a specific choice of next action, such as retrying an example, completing named questions,
  explaining the method aloud, or asking for help.

## After a unit

Include:

- one row per section with confidence and supporting practice-question evidence;
- the skill the student can explain most clearly;
- the error pattern that needs priority;
- corrected work; and
- a next-session plan naming the topic, resource, and task.

Avoid generic prompts such as “How did it go?” Do not ask the student to describe publication
structure, learning design, or authorial choices.
