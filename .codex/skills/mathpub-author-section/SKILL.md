# Write a teaching section

Read both:

- [the section standard](references/section-standard.md)
- [the reader-facing style guide](references/reader-facing-style.md)

Produce a self-contained section with:

1. an engaging introduction addressed to the student;
2. specific “What You Will Learn” outcomes;
3. definitions and topic-specific instruction in a logical sequence;
4. guided examples that show the thought process, intermediate steps, reasons, answer, and check;
5. plausible common mistakes with corrected mathematics;
6. a useful summary; and
7. concrete tips addressed to tutors and homeschool parents.

Use at least four genuinely different examples; use six to eight for a multi-step topic. Connect
symbolic, verbal, graphical, and tabular forms when the topic calls for them.

Reject prose that discusses authors, generation, review, pedagogy, or what “the section” is designed
to do. Outside the parent tips, write to the student rather than about students.

Use $mathpub-author-problem-set and $mathpub-author-self-assessment for the accompanying practice.
