# Algebra 1 curriculum contract

Anna’s partial reference devotes 191 pages to four units and twenty sections. This is a depth
benchmark, not a page-count target: a complete book must preserve comparable explanation,
workbook space, example detail, practice variety, and answer quality instead of compressing more
topics into fewer pages.

## Every section

Require:

- an engaging introduction written to the student;
- three to six specific learning outcomes;
- definitions, notation, and at least two developed explanatory subsections;
- at least four genuinely different guided examples, or six to eight for multi-step material;
- thought process, intermediate steps, reasons, final answer, and checks;
- topic-specific mistakes with incorrect and corrected mathematics;
- a study-useful summary;
- concrete topic-specific tips addressed to tutors and homeschool parents;
- at least twenty distinct mathematical questions with suitable workspace;
- a student-facing self-assessment tied to completed work; and
- a short and explained answer for every question.

Generic prose, repeated examples, padded question counts, and unexplained answer keys fail the
contract even when structural counts pass.

## Every unit

Require:

- five to ten new practice questions from every section;
- at least 20 percent mixed practice without method labels;
- a unit self-assessment tied to practice evidence; and
- complete student-readable solutions.

## Minimum Algebra 1 scope

Include real numbers and expressions; equations and formulas; inequalities and absolute value;
coordinates, relations, functions, domain, and range; linear functions and models; systems;
exponent laws and exponential functions; radicals; polynomial operations and factoring; quadratic
functions and equations; rational expressions; sequences; statistics and probability; and
cumulative modeling.
