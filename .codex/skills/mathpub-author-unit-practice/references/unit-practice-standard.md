# Unit-practice standard

A unit practice is substantial rehearsal, not a short quiz.

## Student practice

- Include five to ten new questions for each section.
- Sample every essential objective and major representation.
- Begin with topic-labelled groups, then reserve at least 20 percent for mixed questions whose
  placement does not reveal the method.
- Include vocabulary and interpretation, routine fluency, multi-step work, error analysis, and
  application or modeling.
- State expectations for work, graph conventions, units, and exact or decimal answers.
- Supply realistic workspace.

Questions must be mathematically new, not copied from section sets or changed only by swapping
coefficients. Mixed questions should require the student to recognize the problem type.

## Answer material

Give every item a short answer and a student-readable worked solution. For graphs, identify
decisive points, boundaries, direction, and inclusion. For models, define variables, form the
relationship, solve, check units and reasonableness, and answer in context.

Finish with a unit self-assessment from $mathpub-author-self-assessment.
