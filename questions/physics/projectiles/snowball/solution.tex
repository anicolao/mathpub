Resolve the launch velocity into components. Returning to the launch height gives
\[
  t=\frac{2v_0\sin\theta}{g}=\boxed{\mpvalue{flight_time}}.
\]
The horizontal velocity is constant, so
\[
  R=(v_0\cos\theta)t
   =\frac{v_0^2\sin(2\theta)}{g}
   =\boxed{\mpvalue{range}}.
\]
At the top, the vertical velocity is zero. Thus
\[
  h_{\max}=\frac{v_0^2\sin^2\theta}{2g}
  =\boxed{\mpvalue{maximum_height}}.
\]
The generator checks the range identity symbolically and verifies that the vertical displacement
at the computed landing time is exactly zero.
