Take upward as positive. Since the roof slopes downward, the initial components are
\[
  v_x=v_0\cos\theta,
  \qquad
  v_y=-v_0\sin\theta.
\]
At impact, vertical position relative to the ground is zero:
\[
  0=h+v_y t-\frac12gt^2.
\]
The positive root gives $t=\mpvalue{impact_time}$. Horizontal motion is uniform, hence
\[
  x=v_xt=\boxed{\mpvalue{impact_distance}}.
\]
The validation record substitutes the impact time back into the vertical equation and checks the
drawn launch angle, impact point, and common axis scale against the generated values.
