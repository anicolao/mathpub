\mpvalue{angle} above the horizontal. It lands at the same height. Ignore air resistance and use
$g=\mpvalue{gravity}$.

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.85]
  \draw[->] (0,0) -- (7.2,0) node[right] {$x$};

  \draw[very thick,->] (0,0) -- (1.8,1.35)
    node[above right] {$v_0=\mpvalue{launch_speed}$};
  \draw (0.9,0) arc[start angle=0,end angle=37,radius=0.9];
  \node at (1.25,0.32) {$\mpvalue{angle}$};
  \draw[dashed,thick,domain=0:6.4,samples=40,smooth]
    plot (\x,{0.92*\x-0.14*\x*\x});
  \fill (0,0) circle (1.7pt);
  \fill (6.4,0) circle (1.7pt) node[below] {landing point};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}

Find (a) the time of flight, (b) the horizontal range, and (c) the maximum height.
