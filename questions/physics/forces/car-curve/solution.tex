Radial acceleration has magnitude $a_{\mathrm c}=v^2/r$. Newton's second law in the inward
direction therefore gives
\[
  F_{\mathrm c}=ma_{\mathrm c}=\mpvalue{force_expression}.
\]
Substitution yields
\[
  F_{\mathrm c}
  =\frac{(\mpvalue{mass})(\mpvalue{speed})^2}{\mpvalue{radius}}
  =\boxed{\mpvalue{force}}.
\]
The force points toward the circle's centre. On a flat road, static friction exerted by the road
on the tires supplies it. The generator checks the symbolic identity, exact substitution, and a
numerical residual independently.
