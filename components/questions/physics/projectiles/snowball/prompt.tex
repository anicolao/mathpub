A snowball rolls off a barn roof that slopes downward at \mpvalue{roof_angle}, as shown. The roof
edge is \mpvalue{barn_height} above the ground, and the snowball's speed as it leaves the roof is
\mpvalue{launch_speed}. Ignore air resistance. How far from the barn does it strike the ground?
Round to one decimal place.

\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.42]

    (\mpvalue{roof_left_x},\mpvalue{roof_left_y});
  \draw[dashed] (\mpvalue{roof_left_x},\mpvalue{barn_height_number}) --
    (\mpvalue{wall_x},\mpvalue{barn_height_number});
  \draw[<->] (1.5,0) -- node[left] {\mpvalue{barn_height}}
    (1.5,\mpvalue{barn_height_number});
  \fill[green!20,draw=black]
    (\mpvalue{wall_x},\mpvalue{barn_height_number}) --
    (\mpvalue{wall_x}-1.1,\mpvalue{barn_height_number})
    arc[start angle=180,end angle={180-\mpvalue{roof_angle_number}},radius=1.1] -- cycle;
  \node at ({\mpvalue{wall_x}-1.7*cos(\mpvalue{roof_angle_number}/2)},
    {\mpvalue{barn_height_number}+1.7*sin(\mpvalue{roof_angle_number}/2)})
    {\mpvalue{roof_angle}};

  \draw[green!50!black,very thick,->]

\end{tikzpicture}
